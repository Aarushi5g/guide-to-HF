My first open source experience

This year I decided that I would participate in hacktoberfest just for fun. I've been thinking about contributing to open source but had no idea where to start and thought that hacktoberfest maybe would help me get started.

TL;DR Resources

My biggest challenge was to find projects and issues I could contribute to. Hacktoberfest is very popular so a lot of issues where taken very quickly.


These resources helped me the most during my hacktoberfest experience

  • Digital Ocean hacktoberfest page
  • Link to Kent C Dodds video series on how to make your first PR
  • A site that help you find projects that needs help
  • A site that help you find good first issues

Other tips

  • Find projects or libraries that you use
  • Look at other peoples PR's to learn how other people solves the issue
  • Clone a repository and play around with the code on your own computer

Preperations

I started research on how to contribute to open source already in the end of september. During that time I read and watched everything on digital oceans resource page about hacktoberfest 2020. Most major repositories have a contribution guide. It is either a website or a markdown document named CONTRIBUTING.MD. For example, React.js or this project Guide-to-HF

I felt a bit overwhelmed. But got good help by these resources:
OpenSourceTogether
This project was very beginner friendly and it was where I did my first contribution. After that I realised that it wasn't that hard to do a PR. Link to repository

Another good resource is a video series on egghead by Kent C. Dodds . Where he showed how github works. Link to video series

Hacktoberfest

October the 1st. Finally! I was pumped, I was ready and I was really looking forward to contributing to some projects. But I found it really hard to find issues that I could take. Most of the issues were taken very quickly. In hindsight I should have looked up issues that I could have contributed to earlier. I had no idea hacktoberfest was so popular.

Anyhow, after hours and hours of looking for issues that I felt I could help out with. I found a few issues, forked the projects and created some PR:s.

I was excited to see if they were going to be approved. But it took a few days and in the end they got approved. I felt very proud and happy to have made "real" contributions to some projects.

Summary

I learnt a lot during hacktoberfest 2020:

  • How to contribute to open source
  • How to get the projects to build on my local computer
  • I got a little better understanding of some projects that I did contribute to and projects I did not contribute to
I have now found one or two projects that I feel that I would like to contribute to regularly when I have the time. The resources at the top helped me the most during hacktober. I hope they will help you to.
